January 21 2022. Friday.

    Today's my last day in Florida. I'm flying back to New York with Jenna (my sister) and Gaius (her cat) tomorrow. I'd meant to drop by the library to get a card (in order to stop leeching off of my dad's Kanopy access) but there was actual work to be done and the day slipped away. I stepped out for a walk around six, heading first for this grassy expanse at the edge of the property, a few leftover acres between the swamp and the single-family houses. It was weirdly quiet, as golden hours go - aside from the crickets, nothing to be heard, save for a grackle hiccuping hoarsely to itself somewhere in the woods. Occasionally a pale little moth would fly from my path, skittering over the grass, too tremulous to get more than an inch or two off the ground. I spent a little time under a big evergreen of some sort, checking the pinecones for seeds the birds and squirrels had missed.

January 22 2022. Saturday.

    → 55/300 (Rock Pigeon)

    Flight back was uneventful (thankfully). Seems that Gaius was pretty calm from arrival at the airport onwards. I dozed off to the third side of Exile on Main St., slept about 40 minutes, and spent my subsequent wakefulness listening to the whole thing. Part of me still wishes the album just consisted of eighteen Rocks Offs, but I do like it. It's got soul – plasticky, but not in the Paul Whiteman sense.

    I was relieved to find that after five weeks my apartment smelled of nothing at all, and that no critters had found the not-quite-hermetically sealed cereal box on the kitchen counter. Shower faucet still leaks. I feel like I should figure out a belated holiday gift for Atilla and Gabor before inquiring about having that fixed. The mailbox was full but not overstuffed, notwithstanding the two Raymond Chandler paperbacks (Tom Adams covers) I'd ordered. Farewell, My Lovely should be coming during the week. After that, the remaining ones are expensive, less well-reputed, or downright impossible to find (The Long Goodbye, unfortunately).

    After unpacking (ten minutes) and loafing about (an hour and a half), I went to get dinner with Robert and Kevin. We brought our orders from 7th Street Burger to their place. 7SB is a very welcome establishment, although their order timing is far from deterministic – Robert got his food almost 10 minutes before Kevin and I did. It was my first chance in a good while to absolutely stuff my face with fast food, so I wisely got two orders of fries. At Robert and Kevin's we stared at the TV for a long while and then fiddled around with Robert's guitar and keyboard a little bit. "Wonderwall" (Rob's fave), "Boulevard of Broken Dreams" (same chords), "Hallelujah" (apparently Robert owes his awareness of this song song to Rufus Wainwright's rendition, and vice versa), and then some weeb crap. I got a carton of milk on the way home and spent an hour or so getting rid of old New Yorkers and maniacally breaking down that Frankensteined cardboard box (half nondescript cardboard, half Pampers packaging) the Portasound keyboard came in.

January 24 2022. Monday.

    Another day mostly devoted to getting my feet back on the ground. I'm going to get a drivers license. Figured out that I need to get a learners permit in this state before doing any coursework, so I guess I'll work on that over the coming days. The other thing I spent a bit of brainpower on is the new bird club – specifically, what to name it. I've already cast aside quite a few ideas, and am currently thinking I might label it simply "Bird Club." I don't think there's a competing generic bird club in the city.

    With Robert out of town, it's apparent how thin my friend network here is. I had stilted conversations with my neighbor and the Village East Square guy today. I think I need to make a real effort to open myself up to social failure again, but thanks to the rona it's too easy to make excuses not to do anything.

    On a more positive note, I had a sudden burst of inspiration regarding what to mail to PLo: a postcard of his nemesis, JLo. Seems so obvious in hindsight.

    Just now I have a strange hankering for a ferry ride. Maybe I'll go to Staten Island tomorrow or something.

January 25 2022. Tuesday.

    Errands, errands... get groceries, return the company WeWork card, print checks to pay the rent, mail said rent, order a new HSA card, trade in my faulty modem, set up the new phone... hopefully within a few more days I'll feel settled. If I follow through with the driving stuff, it might take up just as much time. The hours have once again become difficult to allot.

    Around dinnertime I walked past a large shoot of some sort on Lafayette Street, by Astor Place. I think the action was across the street, but on my side there were several complex arrays of lights and white screens set up to illuminate the facade of the building containing the Blue Man Group theater (and former outpost of "Culture," The Dance). It's perverse, how much work goes into getting something like that to look right. A few months ago I happened upon a shoot for the Sex and the City reboot by Madison Square Park. They did take after take of Sarah Jessica Parker and Bobby Lee strolling about twenty yards, and every time, three extras dressed in athleisure jogged past them and then jogged in place at the street crossing. I've never once seen a person jog in place at an intersection here – or anywhere at all, for that matter.

    I've spent a good few hours on the guitar over the past couple of evenings, mostly trying to learn Mick Taylor's part from this live recording of Rocks Off. I'm proud that I've been able to figure out even part of it by ear. I'm also proud of the immense blister that's appeared on my ring finger, which has borne the brunt of the slides/bends/pulloffs by far. I was still able to play through the discomfort today, but who knows what state of affairs tomorrow will bring. Can't remember if I ever had a blister from cello – I must've at some point.

    Have not worked on my own music since getting back. Largely this is circumstantial but I'm increasingly of the mind to ask Ben to supervise my progress and keep me accountable. Shit just takes so long. I worry that "Popstarred" might be a lost cause with the existing backing track, but the thought of writing a fourth song from scratch is an even bleaker prospect at the moment. One of the few constants in my writing thus far has been my complete inability to write anything worthwhile on the guitar (in terms of words/complete song). Perhaps I should hunker down and really try writing on the Portasound.

January 26 2022. Wednesday.

    Bitterly cold today. I walked to the intersection Mick Jagger yaks about at the beginning of "Dance (Pt. 1)" – "What am I doing standing here on the corner of West 8th Street and 6th Avenue?" Well, 8th St becomes Greenwich Ave, so there's only two corners he could've been talking about ... and the storefronts at those corners are both currently unoccupied. However, I see now that they recorded Emotional Rescue at Electric Lady, which is right around the corner(s) on 8th St. Mystery solved. Foolish of me to embark on an excursion without checking Wikipedia first.

    Anyway, I did need to get my steps in, so from 8th and 6 I looped all the way down to Tribeca. I came across what initially appeared to be some kind of center for promoting democracy in Hong Kong, but in reality was the New York branch office for a Japanese cult whose name rhymes with "Crappy Silence." (There was a lot of signage for praying.) I grabbed a kooky-looking pamphlet, from which my main takeaways thus far are 1) there are over 500 kinds of space people, 2) Star Wars really happened (the original trilogy, at least), and 3) they have a new anime movie out – featuring a character who looks an awful lot like Stan Lee, and whose name is....

    Maybe I should inform his estate.

    I wonder if I can take any inspiration from cult marketing materials for my approach to advertising Bird Club. Or should it be BIRD CLUB? All caps looks nice.

    Lastly, I want to note (for posterity, I guess) that today was the day I figured out that you can check for something extremely gross if you shine your phone flashlight in your mouth and look in a mirror.

January 27 2022. Thursday.

    I got up early enough to work a little on Waiting for Mr. Right. Went to Metrograph this evening to see the print of Sherman's March. It seemed like the projector doesn't really fit in theater 2 or something, and some of the laughter seemed a bit patronizing. But it was really nice to get to see something of McElwee's in theater. Don't think it's as good as Bright Leaves or Time Indefinite, but it's a warmer and cozier watch than either of those, very comfortably low-stakes.

    Pretty burnt out – meant to take a 90 minute nap before the movie, ended up being more than 2 hours. What with the 2.5-hour long movie, I'm glad I got in a little bit of work on WFMR. Every little bit counts.

January 28 2022. Friday.

    It's snowing! I went out for a walk as it began to come down, and was served a frosty reminder of the axiom that no matter which direction I walk in this city, the wind is always blowing in my face. On the way back I tried to get some gin, to entertain myself while being snowed in tomorrow, but the stores I visited didn't have Boodles.

    I can't stall for time any longer on the holiday cards for Attila and Gabor, because I ran into them salting the sidewalk when I got back to the building. What to write....

    All in all, not much to report. The blister has gone away and my fingertips, while still sensitive, have that malleable, pre-callus feel. Time to catch up on sleep.

January 29 2022. Saturday.

    I caught up on sleep here. Had pledged to get some actual work done on WFMR during the snow. Ended up mostly lazing about in bed til like 5, but once I got rolling I actually made a lot of progress – the choruses actually work, and the buzzy bass thing that sealed them also makes sense of the middle instrumental bit with some staccato gating and panning (just figured out you can automate pan instead of having to do some dumb hacky crap with two separate tracks).

January 30 2022. Sunday.

    → 70/300 (American Robin, European Starling, White-throated Sparrow, House Finch, Fox Sparrow, Downy Woodpecker, Canada Goose, Ruddy Duck, LONG-TAILED DUCK, Northern Shoveler, Hooded Merganser, Bufflehead, Wood Duck, Great Black-backed Gull)

    It was a very hard morning to forego the warmth of my bed (relative warmth – my apartment leaks like a church roof) but I thankfully managed to will myself into 3 pairs of pants, into a car, and into Central Park via the entrance on 72nd St by the east side. The snow was really beautiful, the perfect amount/consistency for clomping around in. As I climbed to the Ramble I passed a wide slope covered with sledders and their parents. Sure beats the usual menagerie of ugly-ass dogs off-leash.

    Birdwise, highlight from the Ramble was a pair of the plumpest Fox Sparrows you've ever seen foraging with a bunch of HOSP and WTSP. Apparently there was plenty to eat on top of the snow. I did see a dead fly on top of the powder at the Pinetum. Must have survived the storm but not the cold. The Reservoir was freezing but I could see all the open water was at its north and east, so I tucked in my chin and walked the whole thing. The biggest reward was my first Long-tailed Duck in years and years, in a float of 50 or 60 Ruddy Ducks, bill tucked into wing and glare aimed at onlookers. At the north end there was a little patch of open water with seven duck species, by my count – all the winter residents, even a crisp male Wood Duck.

    I thought about taking another car home, but ended up exiting on the west side around 80th St, grabbing a Levain cookie for lunch, and walking all the way back. This time I walked the southern border of the park after reaching Columbus Circle. I thought the monument from the end of Sherman's March was down there somewhere – it's at the southeastern end, at it turns out. Hence the name Grand Army Plaza. As I remember, it's pretty dull-looking in the movie, but it's an eye-watering shade of gold now thanks to a 2013 restoration and re-gilding.

    After dropping off my binoculars I stepped back out and finally got my Boodles. Didn't have tonic water, but plain old tap water was just fine for my purposes. Got myself into a good mood and went over to bother Robert and Kevin for a few hours in the evening.
